Chapter Summary

"A Friend Found in Music" by Bryanna T. Perkins is a beautiful poem exploring the deep emotional connection between a person and music. Rather than mere entertainment, the poet portrays music as a faithful, therapeutic companion that offers comfort and healing during times of sorrow. Comparing music to an ocean, a rhythm, and a loyal friend, the poem illustrates how its melody touches the deepest parts of the soul.

Whenever the poet feels lonely or sad, music uplifts her spirits and provides the strength to navigate life's challenges. Ultimately, the poem celebrates the transformative power of harmony and rhythm, encouraging readers to find comfort, peace, and lifelong companionship in the comforting embrace of music.

Important Questions and Answers

Short Answer Questions (1–10)

1. Why does the poet compare music to an ocean?

The poet compares music to an ocean because it constantly attracts and comforts her. Like the ocean pulls people towards the shore, music naturally draws the poet closer and fills her heart with peace.

2. What does the expression "moves me to the core" mean?

It means that music deeply touches the poet's emotions. It affects her heart and mind in a powerful and meaningful way.

3. Why does the poet describe music as therapy?

The poet calls music therapy because it comforts her during sadness, removes emotional pain, and gives her hope and happiness.

4. What does the phrase "feel blue" mean?

The phrase "feel blue" means to feel sad, lonely, or unhappy. The poet says music helps her overcome such feelings.

5. How does music lift the poet's spirits?

Music fills the poet with hope, happiness, and confidence. It gives her emotional strength to face difficult situations.

6. Why does the poet call music a "needed friend"?

The poet calls music a needed friend because it always stays with her, supports her emotionally, and never leaves her alone in difficult times.

7. What is the central idea of the poem?

The central idea is that music is a source of comfort, healing, hope, and emotional strength. It becomes a true companion throughout life.

8. Which feeling is expressed throughout the poem?

The poem mainly expresses gratitude, hope, comfort, and deep affection towards music.

9. What message does the poet give through the poem?

The poet teaches that music is more than entertainment. It heals emotional pain, inspires people, and becomes a loyal companion during every stage of life.

10. Why can readers easily relate to this poem?

Readers can relate to the poem because everyone experiences happiness and sadness, and music often provides comfort and emotional support.

Medium Answer Questions (11–20)

11. Explain how music becomes a source of emotional healing for the poet.

The poet describes music as a form of therapy that brings comfort whenever she feels sad. It removes emotional pain, lifts her mood, and gives her hope. Music helps her face life's challenges with courage and confidence, making it an important source of emotional healing.

12. Explain the importance of the metaphors used in the poem.

The poet compares music to an ocean, rhythm, therapy, and a friend. These metaphors help readers understand that music comforts, heals, inspires, and supports people emotionally. They also make the poem more expressive and meaningful.

13. How does music help the poet during difficult times?

Whenever the poet feels unhappy or discouraged, music gives emotional support. It lifts her spirits, removes sadness, and gives her strength to continue moving forward. Music helps her remain hopeful even during challenging situations.

14. Why does the poet consider music a true friend?

The poet believes music is a true friend because it is always available whenever she needs comfort or support. Unlike people who may not always stay nearby, music remains a constant companion in both happy and difficult moments.

15. What does the poem teach us about the power of music?

The poem teaches that music has the power to heal emotional pain, reduce stress, inspire hope, and create happiness. It strengthens the human mind and helps people overcome difficult situations with confidence.

16. Explain the poet's attitude towards music.

The poet's attitude towards music is deeply positive and appreciative. She considers music an essential part of her life because it comforts her during sadness, inspires hope, strengthens her emotionally, and remains a constant source of happiness. The poem reflects the poet's gratitude for the lasting support that music provides.

17. How does repetition strengthen the poem?

The repeated use of the word "Music" emphasises its importance throughout the poem. It keeps the central idea clear and reminds readers that music plays a significant role in every stage of the poet's life. The repetition also adds rhythm and makes the poem more memorable.

18. Describe the mood of the poem with reasons.

The mood of the poem is thoughtful, comforting, and hopeful. The poet reflects on how music supports her during joyful as well as difficult moments. The positive language and emotional expressions create a feeling of peace, confidence, and gratitude.

19. Why is the title "A Friend Found in Music" appropriate?

The title is appropriate because the poem presents music as a loyal and dependable friend. Music comforts the poet during sadness, shares joyful moments, and always remains close whenever emotional support is needed. The title perfectly captures the central idea of the poem.

20. Explain the message conveyed by the poem.

The poem conveys that music has the power to heal emotional pain, inspire hope, reduce loneliness, and bring happiness. It teaches readers that music is not merely entertainment but an important companion that provides strength and comfort throughout life.

Long Answer Questions (21–25)

21. Explain how the poet presents music as an inseparable companion.

Throughout the poem, the poet presents music as a faithful companion who never leaves her alone. She compares music to an ocean, rhythm, therapy, and a needed friend. These comparisons show that music attracts her, touches her emotions, heals sadness, and supports her during difficult times. Whether the poet is happy or sad, music remains by her side. This constant presence makes music one of the most valuable companions in her life.

22. Describe the healing power of music shown in the poem.

The poet explains that music acts like therapy whenever she feels sad or discouraged. It removes negative emotions, lifts her spirits, and fills her heart with hope. Music helps her overcome emotional pain and gives her confidence to face life's challenges. Through these ideas, the poet highlights that music plays an important role in emotional well-being and mental peace.

23. Discuss the poetic devices that make the poem effective.

The poem becomes effective through the use of metaphors, repetition, and simple yet expressive language. Music is compared to an ocean, rhythm, therapy, and a friend, allowing readers to understand its emotional importance. The repeated use of the word "Music" creates emphasis and rhythm. These poetic devices make the poem meaningful, memorable, and emotionally appealing.

24. Explain the main theme of the poem.

The central theme of the poem is the healing and comforting power of music. The poet explains that music removes sadness, creates happiness, gives emotional strength, and acts as a dependable companion. The poem teaches that music can positively influence human emotions and become an important part of everyday life.

25. How does the poem inspire readers to appreciate music?

The poem encourages readers to value music as more than entertainment. It shows that music provides emotional comfort, hope, courage, and companionship during both happy and difficult times. By describing music as a lifelong friend, the poet inspires readers to recognise its positive influence on human life and emotional well-being.
