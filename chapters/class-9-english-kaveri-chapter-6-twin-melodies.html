Chapter Summary

"Twin Melodies" is a thought-provoking play by Mitra Phukan that explores the generational conflict between classical tradition and modern innovation. The story centers around Shruti Sharma, a highly talented young violinist, and her father, Guru Nabin Sharma, a revered Hindustani classical musician who holds conservative views on the purity of art. Shruti dreams of participating in an Indo-Western fusion concert with her friends—Peter, Iqbal, and Avinash. Fearing her father's disapproval, she hesitates to reveal her plans but is encouraged by her friends to speak honestly.

As expected, Guru Nabin initially denies permission, believing fusion weakens the integrity of classical ragas. However, guided by the quiet wisdom of his wife Leela, who reminds him of his own past struggles to pursue the violin against parental wishes, Nabin agrees to witness the group's rehearsal. Listening to their performance, he realizes that the youth have respectfully preserved the essence of the raga while blending it creatively with Western melodies. Overcoming his rigid mindset, Nabin supports Shruti's musical choices. The play highlights how honest communication, empathy, and open-mindedness can bridge generational divides, beautifully demonstrating that tradition and innovation can coexist in perfect harmony.

Important Questions and Answers

Short Answer Questions (1–10)

1. Why was Shruti afraid to tell her father about the fusion concert?

Shruti knew that her father strongly believed in classical music. She feared that he would not approve of her participation in an Indo-Western fusion concert.

2. Who encouraged Shruti to speak honestly with her father?

Peter, Iqbal, and Avinash encouraged Shruti to tell her father the truth instead of hiding the matter from him.

3. What was Guru Nabin Sharma's opinion about fusion music?

He believed that classical Hindustani music should remain pure and felt that fusion music reduced its dignity and originality.

4. How did Leela support Shruti?

Leela advised Shruti to remain truthful and later helped Nabin understand Shruti's feelings by reminding him of his own past experiences.

5. What made Nabin change his opinion?

Watching the rehearsal helped him realise that the fusion performance preserved the beauty of classical music while presenting it creatively.

6. Why did Shruti respect her father's decision even when she disagreed?

She respected her father's knowledge and values. She never wanted to hurt him despite wishing to follow her own musical interests.

7. What lesson from his own life did Nabin remember?

He remembered that he had once chosen the violin against his family's wishes, which helped him understand Shruti's situation.

8. Why is the title Twin Melodies appropriate?

The title symbolises the harmony between classical Indian music and modern musical styles presented together.

9. What qualities of Shruti impress the readers?

Shruti is talented, respectful, honest, patient and determined. She balances her love for music with respect for her family.

10. What message does the play convey?

The play teaches that honest communication, mutual respect and open-mindedness help solve conflicts between generations.

Medium Answer Questions (11–20)

11. Describe Shruti's internal conflict.

Shruti wishes to participate in an Indo-Western fusion concert because she enjoys experimenting with music. However, she knows that her father strongly believes in preserving classical traditions. She respects him deeply but also wants to follow her own passion. This creates a difficult emotional conflict.

12. How do Shruti's friends help her?

Peter, Iqbal and Avinash support Shruti throughout the play. They advise her to speak honestly with her father, encourage her during difficult moments and continue practising sincerely. Their friendship gives Shruti the confidence to face the situation.

13. Explain the role of Leela in resolving the conflict.

Leela acts as the bridge between Shruti and Nabin. She understands both their feelings and encourages honesty. She also reminds Nabin about his own struggle to become a violinist, helping him change his perspective.

14. How does Guru Nabin Sharma change during the play?

Initially, Nabin is rigid and unwilling to accept fusion music. After watching the rehearsal and reflecting on his own past, he realises that creativity can coexist with tradition. He becomes more supportive and open-minded.

15. How does the play promote harmony between tradition and modernity?

The play shows that respecting tradition does not mean rejecting innovation. Shruti successfully combines classical music with modern elements while preserving its essence. The ending highlights that both can exist together harmoniously.

16. How do Peter, Iqbal and Avinash show true friendship?

They support Shruti without forcing her to disobey her parents. Instead, they encourage honesty, patience and confidence. Their positive attitude strengthens Shruti's determination.

17. What role does music play in bringing the family together?

Music becomes the medium through which misunderstandings disappear. After watching the rehearsal, Nabin realises the beauty of Shruti's performance and finally accepts her decision with pride.

18. Why is communication important in the play?

Honest communication removes misunderstandings between Shruti and her father. Once everyone expresses their thoughts openly, the family develops greater trust and understanding.

19. What does Nabin's final decision reveal about his character?

His decision shows that he is willing to learn from his mistakes. He values his daughter's happiness and realises that supporting her dreams does not weaken tradition.

20. What values can students learn from Twin Melodies?

Students learn the importance of honesty, respect, perseverance, family values, friendship, acceptance and balancing tradition with innovation.

Long Answer Questions (21–25)

21. Describe the conflict between Shruti and Guru Nabin Sharma.

The central conflict of the play arises because Shruti wishes to participate in an Indo-Western fusion concert, while her father, Guru Nabin Sharma, believes that classical Hindustani music should remain pure and unchanged. Shruti respects her father's opinions but also wants the freedom to explore new musical ideas. This difference in viewpoints creates tension within the family. Eventually, through honest communication and understanding, the conflict is resolved peacefully.

22. Explain how Guru Nabin Sharma changes by the end of the play.

At the beginning of the play, Guru Nabin Sharma strongly opposes fusion music because he believes it may harm classical traditions. However, after watching Shruti's rehearsal, he realises that the students have preserved the beauty of the classical raga while presenting it creatively. Leela also reminds him of his own struggles as a young musician. This makes him more open-minded, and he finally supports Shruti's decision.

23. Discuss the role of Leela in resolving the family conflict.

Leela plays an important role as a peacemaker in the family. She understands both Shruti's dreams and Nabin's concerns. Instead of taking sides, she encourages honesty and patience. She reminds Nabin about his own experience of choosing the violin despite his family's objections. Her wisdom helps Nabin understand Shruti's feelings, leading to reconciliation and harmony in the family.

24. Explain the significance of the title Twin Melodies.

The title Twin Melodies symbolises the coming together of two different musical traditions—classical Indian music and modern Western music. It also represents harmony between two generations and two different ways of thinking. The title reflects the main idea that tradition and innovation can exist together without losing their identity.

25. What values does the play teach students?

The play teaches several important values such as honesty, respect, understanding, open-mindedness and family support. It encourages students to respect tradition while welcoming positive change. It also shows that meaningful communication can solve misunderstandings and that parents and children should trust each other. These values make the play meaningful as well as inspiring.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why was Shruti afraid to tell her father about the fusion concert?

She feared he would disapprove because he was a conservative classical musician who strongly opposed fusion.

Who wrote the play 'Twin Melodies'?

The play was written by Mitra Phukan, focusing on the blend of traditional values and modern musical ideas.

How did Leela help resolve the conflict?

She advised Shruti to be honest and reminded Nabin of his own past rebellion to play the violin, softening his rigid stance.

What is the central theme of Twin Melodies?

The central theme is the harmonious coexistence of tradition and innovation, bridged by mutual respect and honest communication.

Why is the title 'Twin Melodies' appropriate?

It symbolises both the harmonious blending of Indian classical and Western music, and the bridging of generational differences.

What changed Guru Nabin Sharma's mind?

Seeing the dedication and respect in the youth's rehearsal proved to him that fusion did not ruin the purity of classical raga, but celebrated it creatively.
