Poem Summary

"I Cannot Remember My Mother" is a beautiful, nostalgic poem by Rabindranath Tagore. The poet, having lost his mother in early childhood, cannot recall her physical face or presence. However, her memory remains eternally alive in his subconscious, triggered by simple daily sensations.

First, while playing with his toys, a soft tune hovers over them, reminding him of the songs she hummed while rocking his cradle. Second, during autumn, the sweet fragrance of shiuli flowers and the scent of morning temple prayers bring back her loving presence. Finally, looking out his window at the vast, peaceful blue sky, he feels his mother's gentle, protective gaze watching over him from above.

Tagore uses rich sensory imagery to show how love transcends physical death. The poem highlights the everlasting bond between a mother and child, illustrating that those we lose live on through the sounds, scents, and sights of nature.

Important Questions and Answers

Short Answer Questions (1–10)

1. Who is the poet of "I Cannot Remember My Mother"?

The poem is written by Rabindranath Tagore, a renowned Indian poet, writer, and Nobel Prize winner.

2. What line is repeated in every stanza?

The line "I cannot remember my mother" is repeated at the beginning of each stanza as a refrain.

3. What reminds the poet of his mother while he is playing?

A tune that seems to hover over his toys reminds him of the songs his mother used to hum.

4. Which flower is mentioned in the poem?

The poet mentions the shiuli flower, known for its sweet fragrance during autumn mornings.

5. Which sense is associated with the shiuli flowers?

The sense of smell is associated with the fragrance of the shiuli flowers.

6. What does the scent of the temple service remind the poet of?

The scent reminds him of his mother and her loving presence in his life.

7. What does the poet see through his bedroom window?

He sees the distant blue sky through his bedroom window.

8. How does the poet connect the sky with his mother?

He feels that the stillness of his mother's loving gaze has spread across the sky.

9. What is the mood of the poem?

The mood of the poem is nostalgic, affectionate, and emotional.

10. What is the main theme of the poem?

The poem highlights the everlasting love between a mother and her child and the power of memories.

Medium Answer Questions (11–20)

11. Why does the poet say that he cannot remember his mother?

The poet cannot clearly remember his mother's appearance because she is no longer physically present in his life. However, he still remembers her through various sensory experiences and emotions.

12. How does music help the poet remember his mother?

While playing, the poet hears a tune that reminds him of the songs his mother hummed while rocking his cradle. The melody brings back loving memories of her care and affection.

13. Explain the significance of the shiuli flowers.

The fragrance of the shiuli flowers serves as a symbol of the mother's presence. Their sweet scent awakens memories and emotions connected with her love.

14. How does the poet use the sense of smell in the poem?

The poet uses the fragrance of shiuli flowers and the scent of the temple service to trigger memories of his mother. These smells help him feel emotionally connected to her.

15. What does the blue sky symbolize?

The blue sky symbolizes the eternal and protective presence of the poet's mother. It reflects her calm and loving gaze.

16. Explain the role of imagery in the poem.

The poet uses auditory imagery through the tune, olfactory imagery through the fragrance of flowers and temple scents, and visual imagery through the blue sky. These images make the poem vivid and emotional.

17. What is the significance of the refrain in the poem?

The repeated line emphasizes the poet's inability to remember his mother directly while highlighting the different ways her memory survives in his mind.

18. How does nature help the poet remember his mother?

Natural elements such as flowers, fragrance, and the sky act as reminders of the poet's mother. Nature keeps her memory alive in his heart.

19. What emotions are expressed in the poem?

The poem expresses love, longing, affection, nostalgia, and deep emotional attachment to the poet's mother.

20. What message does the poem convey?

The poem conveys that true love and memories never disappear. Loved ones continue to live in our hearts through memories and experiences.

Long Answer Questions (21–25)

21. How does the poet remember his mother through different sensory experiences?

The poet remembers his mother through sound, smell, and sight. While playing, a tune reminds him of the songs she used to hum. The fragrance of shiuli flowers and the scent of temple prayers bring back memories of her loving presence. When he looks at the blue sky, he imagines her calm gaze spread across it. These sensory experiences help him keep her memory alive even though he cannot remember her clearly.

22. Discuss the use of imagery in the poem.

Imagery is an important feature of the poem. Auditory imagery is created through the humming tune. Olfactory imagery appears through the fragrance of shiuli flowers and temple scents. Visual imagery is created through the image of the blue sky and the mother's gaze. These images make the poet's memories vivid and meaningful.

23. What does the poem reveal about the bond between a mother and child?

The poem reveals that the bond between a mother and child is eternal. Even though the poet cannot clearly remember his mother, her love continues to influence his thoughts and emotions. Her memory remains alive through everyday experiences and feelings.

24. Explain the significance of the title "I Cannot Remember My Mother".

The title expresses the poet's inability to recall his mother directly. However, as the poem develops, readers understand that her memory survives through music, fragrance, and nature. The title highlights both loss and remembrance.

25. Describe the central theme of the poem.

The central theme of the poem is the enduring power of love and memory. The poet shows that even when a loved one is absent, memories remain alive through sounds, scents, sights, and emotions. The poem celebrates the everlasting bond between a mother and her child.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does the poet say he cannot remember his mother?

The poet lost his mother at an early age, so he has no clear visual image of her face. However, her memory is triggered by sensory perceptions like a lullaby, autumn scents, and the blue sky.

What reminds the poet of his mother while playing with toys?

A faint, soft tune seems to hover over his playthings, reminding him of a comforting lullaby she used to hum when she rocked his cradle.

Which flowers bring back memories of his mother?

The sweet-scented shiuli flowers of early autumn mornings, whose fragrance carries the atmosphere of morning temple services and her devotion.

What does the poet see when looking at the distant sky?

Looking at the calm expanse from his bedroom window, he feels his mother's silent, protective, and loving gaze has spread across the entire blue sky.

What is the main message of the poem?

The poem teaches that true love transcends physical absence; the maternal bond is eternal, surviving through daily sounds, scents, and sights of nature.

Who is the poet of I Cannot Remember My Mother?

The poem is written by Rabindranath Tagore, the celebrated Nobel laureate.
