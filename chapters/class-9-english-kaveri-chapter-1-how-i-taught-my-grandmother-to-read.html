Chapter Summary

"How I Taught My Grandmother to Read" is an inspiring story written by Sudha Murty. The story highlights the importance of education and proves that learning has no age limit.

The narrator, a twelve-year-old girl, lived with her grandparents in a village in North Karnataka. During that time, newspapers and magazines reached the village late because transportation facilities were limited. The narrator's grandmother, Krishtakka, loved listening to the serial story Kashi Yatre written by the famous Kannada author Triveni. Since she could not read, the narrator used to read each episode aloud to her every week.

The grandmother became deeply attached to the story because she identified herself with the old lady in the novel. She eagerly waited every week to know what happened next. One day, the narrator went to attend a wedding in a nearby village and stayed there for a week. During her absence, a new episode of Kashi Yatre arrived. The grandmother could see the pictures but could not read the text. She felt helpless and dependent because she had to rely on others to know the story.

When the narrator returned, she found her grandmother in tears. The grandmother explained how painful it felt to be unable to read. She revealed that she had never attended school because, in those days, girls were not encouraged to study. Determined to become independent, she decided to learn the Kannada alphabet and set a goal to read a novel by herself before Saraswati Puja during the Dassara festival.

The narrator became her teacher. The grandmother worked very hard every day. She practised reading, writing, and memorising lessons with great dedication. Her determination and discipline helped her learn quickly.

On the day of Saraswati Puja, the grandmother successfully read the title of the novel on her own. As a mark of respect, she touched the feet of her granddaughter, not as her granddaughter but as her teacher. The narrator felt emotional and proud.

The story teaches that education is a powerful tool for independence and self-respect. It also shows that age is never a barrier to learning. With determination and hard work, anyone can achieve their goals.

Important Questions and Answers

Short Answer Questions (1–10)

1. Why did the grandmother love Kashi Yatre?

She identified herself with the old lady in the story and eagerly wanted to know what happened next.

2. Why could the grandmother not read?

She never attended school because girls' education was not considered important during her childhood.

3. Why was the grandmother upset when the narrator returned?

She could not read the latest episode of the story and felt helpless and dependent.

4. What goal did the grandmother set for herself?

She wanted to learn the Kannada alphabet and read a novel independently before Saraswati Puja.

5. Why did the narrator laugh at her grandmother?

She thought learning at the age of sixty-two would be difficult.

6. How did the grandmother feel about being illiterate?

She felt embarrassed, dependent, and helpless.

7. Who became the grandmother's teacher?

Her twelve-year-old granddaughter became her teacher.

8. What did the grandmother do to learn quickly?

She practised reading, writing, repeating, and memorising lessons regularly.

9. Why did the grandmother touch the narrator's feet?

She wanted to show respect to her teacher.

10. What is the main message of the story?

Learning has no age limit and education brings independence.

Medium Answer Questions (11–20)

11. Why was Kashi Yatre important to the grandmother?

The grandmother related deeply to the old woman in the novel. She eagerly waited for every new episode. The story became an important part of her life and daily discussions with friends.

12. What happened when the narrator went to a wedding?

The narrator stayed away for a week. During this time, a new episode arrived. The grandmother could not read it and felt helpless, which motivated her to become literate.

13. How did the grandmother realise the value of education?

She realised its value when she could not read the story herself. This experience made her understand how important literacy is for independence.

14. Describe the grandmother's determination.

She fixed a clear deadline, studied regularly, completed homework sincerely, and practised daily. Her strong determination helped her succeed.

15. Why did the grandmother want to become independent?

She did not want to depend on others for reading. She believed education would give her confidence and self-respect.

16. How did the narrator help her grandmother?

The narrator taught her the Kannada alphabet, gave regular lessons, and encouraged her throughout the learning process.

17. What qualities of the grandmother impress you most?

Her determination, discipline, humility, courage, and willingness to learn despite old age are highly inspiring.

18. How does the story highlight women's education?

The story shows how earlier generations of women missed educational opportunities. It emphasises that education is essential for everyone.

19. Why was Saraswati Puja chosen as the deadline?

Saraswati is the goddess of learning and knowledge. The grandmother considered the occasion sacred and motivating.

20. What role did the granddaughter play in the grandmother's success?

She acted as a caring teacher, guide, and supporter. Her efforts helped the grandmother achieve her dream.

Long Answer Questions (21–25)

21. Describe the relationship between the narrator and her grandmother.

The narrator and her grandmother shared a loving and affectionate bond. The grandmother trusted her granddaughter deeply and enjoyed listening to stories read by her. When she decided to learn reading, she approached her granddaughter for help. The narrator accepted the responsibility and taught her patiently. Their relationship became even stronger through the process of learning. The mutual respect and affection between them make the story touching and memorable.

22. How did the grandmother transform herself through education?

Initially, the grandmother depended on others for reading. After feeling helpless during the narrator's absence, she decided to learn. She worked hard every day and mastered the Kannada alphabet. Eventually, she could read independently. Education transformed her from a dependent person into a confident and self-reliant individual.

23. Explain the significance of the title "How I Taught My Grandmother to Read."

The title reflects the central event of the story. A young girl becomes a teacher to her elderly grandmother. It highlights the reversal of traditional roles and shows that knowledge is more important than age. The title also emphasises the importance of education and lifelong learning.

24. What values do we learn from the grandmother's character?

The grandmother teaches us determination, perseverance, humility, respect for teachers, and self-confidence. She proves that age should never stop a person from learning. Her willingness to work hard and achieve her goal inspires readers to overcome challenges.

25. Why is education important according to this story?

Education empowers people and makes them independent. It helps individuals gain confidence, self-respect, and freedom from dependence on others. The story shows that literacy is not only about reading and writing but also about dignity and personal growth. Therefore, education is essential at every stage of life.
