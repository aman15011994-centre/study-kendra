How to Calculate Attendance Percentage

Calculating your attendance percentage is simple. Use this formula:

Formula: Attendance % = (Classes Attended ÷ Total Classes Held) × 100

Example 1 — Basic Attendance Calculation

You attended 45 classes out of 60 total classes held.
Attendance % = (45 ÷ 60) × 100 = 75% — exactly at the minimum required limit.

Example 2 — How Many Classes Can I Bunk?

Formula: Safe Bunks = Classes Attended - (Target % ÷ 100 × Total Classes)

You attended 80 out of 100 classes (80%). Target is 75%.
Safe Bunks = 80 - (0.75 × 100) = 80 - 75 = 5 classes you can safely bunk.

Example 3 — Classes Needed to Reach 75%

Formula: Classes Needed = (Target % × Total Classes - 100 × Classes Attended) ÷ (100 - Target %)

You attended 50 out of 90 classes (55.5%). Target is 75%.
Classes Needed = (75 × 90 - 100 × 50) ÷ (100 - 75) = (6750 - 5000) ÷ 25 = 1750 ÷ 25 = 70 consecutive classes without bunking.

Frequently Asked Questions

How to calculate attendance percentage?

Divide classes attended by total classes held, then multiply by 100. Example: 45 attended out of 60 total = (45 ÷ 60) × 100 = 75%.

How many classes can I bunk and still maintain 75%?

If your attendance is above 75%, subtract your minimum required classes from your attended classes. Example: 80 attended out of 100 total — safe bunks = 80 - 75 = 5. Our calculator shows this automatically.

What happens if attendance is below 75%?

Most Indian colleges detain students with less than 75% attendance, meaning they cannot appear in semester exams. Some colleges may allow exams with a medical certificate but deduct internal marks.

How many classes do I need to attend to reach 75%?

This depends on your current attendance and total classes. Enter your numbers in the calculator above and it will instantly tell you how many consecutive classes you need to attend without bunking.

Is 75% attendance compulsory in India?

Yes, 75% attendance is mandatory in most CBSE schools, state board colleges, and universities in India. Some institutions require 80% or even 85%. Always check your college's specific attendance policy.
