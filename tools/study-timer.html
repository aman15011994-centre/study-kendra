What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a simple and proven time management method that helps students study more effectively without burnout.

Basic Cycle: Study 25 min → Break 5 min → Repeat 4 times → Long Break 15-30 min

Step 1 — Pick One Topic

Before starting the timer, decide exactly what you will study. Example: "Chapter 5 — Laws of Motion, solve 10 numericals." One clear goal per session.

Step 2 — Set 25 Minutes and Focus

Click "Pomodoro 25m" above and start. No phone, no social media, no distractions for exactly 25 minutes. If a distraction thought comes, write it down and come back to it after the session.

Step 3 — Take a Short Break

When the timer rings, stop immediately. Click "Short Break 5m" and genuinely rest — stretch, drink water, look away from screen. Do not study during breaks.

Step 4 — Repeat and Take a Long Break

After every 4 Pomodoros (2 hours of focused study), take a 15-30 minute long break. Eat something, go for a short walk. Your brain needs real recovery before the next block.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

Study 25 minutes with full focus, then take a 5-minute break. Repeat 4 times, then take a 15-30 minute long break. One 25-minute session is called one Pomodoro. It prevents burnout and improves concentration.

How long should I study in one session?

25 minutes is ideal for most students. For competitive exams like JEE and NEET, you can extend to 45-50 minutes per session. Never study more than 90 minutes without a proper break — your retention drops sharply.

Does the Pomodoro Technique work for JEE and NEET?

Yes, highly effective. Target 12-16 Pomodoros per day (5-7 hours of focused study) with proper breaks. Assign each Pomodoro to one specific topic or chapter for better tracking and coverage.

How many Pomodoros should I do in a day?

8-12 Pomodoros (3-5 hours) is good for school students. Competitive exam aspirants can do 12-16 Pomodoros (5-7 hours). Quality of focus matters more than quantity — 8 focused Pomodoros beat 16 distracted ones.
